### Stay Safe Online: 10 Essential Do's and Don'ts at Kenyan Cyber Cafés

**H1:** Cyber Café Internet Safety: How to Protect Your Data in Nairobi CBD

Using public WiFi at cyber cafés? Be careful! Hackers are everywhere, and public computers can be risky. At **Skyteey Computers in Nairobi CBD**, we prioritize your security. Here's what you absolutely must know about staying safe online.

---

**H2:** Why Cyber Café Safety Matters

Every day, thousands of Kenyans use cyber cafés to:
- Check emails
- Apply for jobs
- Manage bank accounts
- Handle government services
- Access sensitive documents

This makes cyber cafés targets for criminals. **Don't be a victim** – follow these guidelines!

---

**H2:** 5 ESSENTIAL DO'S ✅

**H3:** DO #1: Use a VPN (Virtual Private Network)
- VPNs encrypt your data
- Makes it nearly impossible for hackers to see what you're doing
- Download free apps like Hotspot Shield or ProtonVPN before visiting
- **Why it matters:** Protects your passwords and sensitive info

**H3:** DO #2: Log Out Completely
- Never just close the browser
- Click "Sign Out" or "Log Off"
- Clear browsing history before leaving
- Delete cookies if possible
- **Why it matters:** Prevents next user from accessing your accounts

**H3:** DO #3: Use Strong, Unique Passwords
- Passwords like "123456" or "password" are useless
- Use mix of letters, numbers, symbols
- Different passwords for different sites
- Consider password manager apps
- **Why it matters:** Weak passwords are cracked in seconds

**H3:** DO #4: Verify Website Security
- Look for "HTTPS://" (not just "HTTP://")
- Check for padlock icon in address bar
- Avoid entering passwords on unsecured sites
- Legitimate banks always use HTTPS
- **Why it matters:** HTTPS encrypts your connection

**H3:** DO #5: Enable Two-Factor Authentication
- Adds extra security layer to accounts
- Requires second verification (usually SMS or app code)
- Available on Gmail, Facebook, WhatsApp, banking apps
- Takes 2 minutes to set up
- **Why it matters:** Even with stolen password, accounts stay protected

---

**H2:** 5 CRITICAL DON'Ts ❌

**H3:** DON'T #1: Access Banking Online at Cyber Cafés
- Cybercafés = High-risk for money theft
- Wait until you're on your personal device at home
- If absolutely necessary, use VPN + strong password
- Check account afterward for unauthorized transactions
- **Why it matters:** Your money is more important than convenience

**H3:** DON'T #2: Save Passwords in Browsers
- When you click "Save password," browsers store it
- Next user can access your accounts
- Especially dangerous for email and banking
- Manually type password each time
- **Why it matters:** Protects you from account theft

**H3:** DON'T #3: Use Public WiFi Without VPN
- Hackers intercept unencrypted data easily
- Your passwords and messages exposed
- Government websites, banking apps – all vulnerable
- Always use VPN before connecting
- **Why it matters:** Prevents "man-in-the-middle" attacks

**H3:** DON'T #4: Download Files from Untrusted Sources
- USB drives from strangers = malware risk
- Unsafe download links = viruses
- Only download from official websites
- Scan any USB with antivirus first
- **Why it matters:** Malware steals data and ruins devices

**H3:** DON'T #5: Leave Your Device Unattended
- Never leave phone/laptop unlocked while you step away
- Take it with you or lock the screen
- Someone could access your private information
- Don't trust "just for a minute"
- **Why it matters:** Physical theft is real and quick

---

**H2:** At Skyteey Computers: We Protect Your Safety

✅ **Regular security updates** on all computers
✅ **Antivirus software** running continuously
